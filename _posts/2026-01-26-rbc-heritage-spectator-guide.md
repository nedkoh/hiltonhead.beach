The 58th RBC Heritage Presented by Boeing returns to the famed Harbour Town Golf Links from April 13-19, 2026. As one of nine PGA TOUR Signature Events, this tournament features a $20 million purse, a limited field of elite players, and a no-cut format that guarantees star power all weekend. Here is everything you need to know to make the most of your RBC Heritage experience.

## Tournament Overview

The RBC Heritage has been a Hilton Head tradition since 1969, when Arnold Palmer won the inaugural event. Played the week after The Masters, it attracts top players looking to continue their momentum or rebound from Augusta disappointment.

**2026 Tournament Details:**
- **Dates:** April 13-19, 2026
- **Location:** Harbour Town Golf Links, Sea Pines Resort
- **Purse:** $20 million
- **Format:** Signature Event (limited field, no cut)
- **Defending Champion:** Check official website for latest

The recently restored Harbour Town course, which reopened in November 2025 following Davis Love III's comprehensive renovation, will provide a refreshed challenge for the world's best players.

## Ticket Options and Pricing

### Practice Round Tickets (Tuesday-Wednesday)
Practice round tickets provide single-day access to tournament grounds. These are excellent for:
- First-time attendees wanting a relaxed experience
- Getting autographs (players are more accessible)
- Enjoying the Opening Ceremonies (Tuesday)
- Watching the Pro-Am (Wednesday)

**Note:** The course is closed to spectators on Monday.

### Daily Grounds Tickets (Thursday-Sunday)
Standard tournament admission for competition rounds. Early arrival is recommended for the best viewing positions.

### Premium Hospitality Options

**Calibogue Club**
An all-inclusive hospitality venue open Thursday through Sunday, located between the 17th green and 18th tee with a satellite location at the 8th green.

- Climate-controlled premium atmosphere
- Complimentary lunch buffet (11:00 AM - 6:00 PM)
- Full-service bar
- Outdoor stadium seating with prime views

**Lighthouse Club**
Offers spectacular views of the final putts on the signature 18th green, right beside the iconic Harbour Town Lighthouse.

**VIP Tickets**
The "everywhere you want to go" pass includes access to:
- Calibogue Club
- Lighthouse Club
- Doc's BBQ at 15
- Full tournament grounds
- On-site parking (for every two tickets purchased)

### Special Ticket Policies

- **Juniors:** Children 15 and under are admitted FREE when accompanied by a ticketed adult
- **Young Children:** Kids under 5 do not need tickets for shared hospitality venues; ages 6-15 need venue tickets but must be accompanied by adults
- **Military:** Service members receive two complimentary tickets for the Wednesday practice round, plus 20% off Thursday-Sunday grounds tickets

**Contact:** For questions, call (843) 671-2448 or email tickets@heritageclassicfoundation.com

## Parking and Transportation

### Important: No On-Site Parking
There is no spectator parking at Harbour Town. All attendees must use designated off-site lots and shuttle services.

### Primary Parking Location
**Coastal Discovery Museum at Honey Horn**
- Address: 70 Honey Horn Drive, Hilton Head Island, SC 29926
- Cost: Free
- Shuttle: Complimentary motor coach to Harbour Town Marina
- Shuttle Time: Approximately 20 minutes each way
- Hours: 6:30 AM - 9:30 PM (Tuesday through Sunday)

### Bicycle Parking
Complimentary bicycle parking is available inside Sea Pines at the tennis courts near Harbour Town Tennis Parking.

**Cyclist Entry Points:**
- Ocean Gate on South Forest Beach Drive
- Main Gate on Greenwood Drive

**Requirements:** All cyclists must show a digital ticket or volunteer badge for Sea Pines entry.

**Important:** E-bikes are not allowed in Sea Pines.

### ADA Accessible Parking
ADA-accessible parking with shuttle service is available. A valid handicap placard is required.

## Best Viewing Spots

### Signature Locations

**18th Green and Lighthouse**
The iconic finishing hole is a must-see. The Lighthouse Club provides premium viewing, but general admission spectators can find spots along the fairway and around the green.

**Par 3 Holes**
Fan favorites for their compact viewing areas:
- 4th Hole
- 7th Hole
- 14th Hole
- 17th Hole

**Practice Facilities**
During practice rounds, the driving range and putting green offer close-up views of players warming up.

### Viewing Strategy Tips

- **Arrive Early:** The best spots fill up quickly, especially on weekends
- **Follow Groups:** Pick your favorite players and walk the course with them
- **Camp Out:** Find a comfortable spot at a key hole and watch groups come through
- **Use Grandstands:** Stadium seating at select holes provides excellent elevated views

## Food and Beverage

### Concession Options

The SERG Restaurant Group operates specialty concession stands throughout the course, offering elevated fare beyond typical tournament food.

**Key Dining Locations:**

- **Fraser's at the Pavilion:** Open to all spectators, located under the Clubhouse adjacent to the 10th tee
- **Doc's BBQ at 15:** Southern barbecue near the 15th hole (VIP access)
- **Michelob Ultra Club:** Food and beverages on the Heritage Lawn
- **Tito's Golf Club:** Cocktails on the Heritage Lawn
- **Specialty Cocktail Venue:** Premium tequila cocktails for adults

### Tips for Dining
- Eat during mid-morning or mid-afternoon to avoid peak lunch crowds
- Bring cash for faster transactions at some locations
- Stay hydrated - purchase water early and refill throughout the day

## What to Bring (and What to Leave Behind)

### Recommended Items
- Comfortable walking shoes (you will cover several miles)
- Sunscreen and hat
- Sunglasses
- Light jacket or rain gear (April weather can vary)
- Small binoculars
- Smartphone (for leaderboard updates and photos)
- Portable phone charger
- Small folding chair (check current policy)

### Prohibited Items
- Large bags or backpacks (check size restrictions)
- Professional cameras with detachable lenses
- Outside food and beverages
- Pets (except service animals)
- E-bikes

## On-Site Amenities

### Liberty Oak Expo
Located in the Marina next to the Harbour Town Lighthouse, this area features display tents where spectators can view and interact with sponsor products.

### Merchandise
- **Main Pavilion:** Located between the 1st and 9th holes
- **Pop-up Tents:** Heritage Lawn and Liberty Oak areas

Pro tip: Shop early in the week for the best selection, especially for popular items and sizes.

### Cool Zone
Located between holes 1 and 9, featuring:
- Large video board for watching action around the course
- Golf viewing areas
- Shaded seating

## Insider Tips for the Best Experience

1. **Tuesday's Opening Ceremonies** offer a festive atmosphere with less crowding than competition days

2. **Wednesday's Pro-Am** lets you see celebrities and local personalities paired with professionals

3. **Walk the back nine** during afternoon rounds when the leaderboard drama intensifies

4. **Dress in layers** - April mornings can be cool, but afternoons warm up

5. **Download the PGA TOUR app** for real-time scoring and player tracking

6. **Plan your exit** - Leave 15-30 minutes before you want to catch your shuttle to avoid crowds
